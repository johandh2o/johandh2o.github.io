About me

I am currently pursuing my PhD at the Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (MatNat) at the University of Oslo (UiO), under the supervision of Dr. Johan Pensar. Additionally, I hold a research position at the Norwegian Institute of Public Health (NIPH), where I am supervised by Dr. Dr. Guido Biele.

My PhD research project focuses on estimating the causal effect of pharmacological treatment for attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) upon school performance using observational data from Norwegian schoolchildren.

I possess undergraduate degrees in industrial engineering and economics from Universidad de los Andes (UniAndes) in Colombia. Furthermore, I have obtained master’s degrees in economics and statistics, the latter of which was awarded by Michigan State University (MSU) where I was an international Fulbright scholar.

My professional experience includes working as a research consultant and lead data scientist. In these roles, I have contributed to projects focused on impact evaluation, regional competitiveness, international trade, mobile banking, and alternative credit scoring.

Research

My research interests and experience encompass:

  • Graphical and nonparametric methods for identifying causal queries.

  • Semiparametric inference with valid uncertainty quantification.

  • Applications that bridge econometrics, biostatistics, game theory, and network science.
